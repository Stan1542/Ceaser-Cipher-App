What is Ceaser Cipher

The Caesar Cipher, named after Julius Caesar, is a basic encryption technique where each letter in the plaintext is shifted a fixed number of positions down or up the alphabet. Despite its simplicity and susceptibility to modern cryptographic attacks, it holds educational value for teaching encryption concepts, is used in puzzles and games for entertainment, offers insights into historical cryptography, and can serve in low-security scenarios or casual communication as a rudimentary encryption method.

What is Encryption and Decryption

Encryption is the process of converting plaintext or data into an encoded format, known as ciphertext, using an algorithm and a key. It ensures that the original data cannot be understood or accessed without the corresponding decryption key. Decryption, on the other hand, is the reverse process of encryption, where the ciphertext is converted back into its original plaintext form using the decryption key. Encryption and decryption are fundamental techniques in cryptography, used to secure sensitive information during transmission or storage, protecting it from unauthorized access or interception.
